Ansible vs. Traditional Scripting

🚀

Agentless Architecture

Ansible's greatest strength. It uses standard OpenSSH for transport, meaning there is no "agent" to upgrade, no extra ports to open, and no background service to crash on target nodes.

⚖

Idempotency & Desired State

Unlike traditional scripting, Ansible is "State-Aware." It focuses on bringing a system to a predefined state, regardless of its starting point. This reduces "Configuration Drift."

📡

Push vs. Pull Dynamics

Most automation tools use a pull model. Ansible's push model gives the administrator full control over when updates happen, allowing for coordinated "rolling updates".
